About

The studio behind the curtain

La Seine Studio started the way most stories do — small, unfinished, and a little obsessive about getting one scene exactly right.

Why we tell stories this way

Every story here starts as a sketch on the back of something else — a napkin, a script margin, a sleepless thought. We keep the rough edges in, because that's where the feeling usually is.

We're not chasing polish for its own sake. We're chasing the moment a frame stops looking animated and starts looking felt.

The Process

From margin note to moving frame

A story usually begins as a single image or a single line of dialogue. From there: a logline, a cast of characters, rough tests, and a lot of redrawing the same three seconds.

Nothing leaves this stage until it feels true — not just technically correct.
